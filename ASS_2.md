# SDLC Review Questions and Answers

## 1. What is SDLC?
SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) is a systematic process used to develop software from planning to maintenance.

## 2. Why is SDLC important?
SDLC is important because it helps ensure that software is developed efficiently, meets user requirements, and maintains quality.

## 3. List the seven phases of SDLC.
- Planning  
- Requirements Analysis  
- System Design  
- Development  
- Testing  
- Deployment  
- Maintenance  

## 4. Differentiate between functional and non-functional requirements.
Functional requirements describe **what the system should do**, while non-functional requirements describe **how the system should perform**, such as speed, reliability, and security.

## 5. What happens during the testing phase?
During the testing phase, the software is checked to identify and correct defects and to ensure it meets specified requirements.

## 6. Differentiate between deployment and maintenance.
Deployment is the process of releasing and installing software for users, while maintenance involves fixing errors, updating features, and improving the system after deployment.

## 7. Compare the Waterfall and Agile models.
- **Waterfall Model:** A linear and sequential approach where each phase is completed before the next begins.  
- **Agile Model:** An iterative and flexible approach that allows continuous feedback and frequent updates during development.

## 8. Who are the major stakeholders involved in SDLC?
- Project Manager  
- Business Analyst  
- UI/UX Designer  
- Software Developer  
- Quality Assurance Engineer  
- System Administrator (DevOps)  
- End Users  

## 9. What is User Acceptance Testing (UAT)?
User Acceptance Testing (UAT) is the testing performed by end users to confirm that the software meets their requirements and is ready for deployment.

## 10. Why is maintenance important after deployment?
Maintenance is important because it fixes bugs, improves performance, enhances security, and ensures the software continues to meet user needs.
